Research

Our research interests lies in developing novel methods for pathogen discovery and diagnosis related research, as well as using these methods to investigate clinical/zoonotic infectious diseases, the etiology of unexplained diseases, and relavent potential pathogenic mechanisms research. To date, we have published 40+ SCI research paper with 3000+ citations.

Our previous work include the invention and advancement of pathogen capture sequencing technology and high-throughput Serochip technology; developed BacCapSeq, which is the first platform that can simultaneously screen for all human pathogenic bacteria, virulence, and antibiotic resistance markers; first discovered that SARS-CoV-2 viral RNA can persist in patients' digestive tracts even months after being eliminated from their respiratory tracts; conducted molecular epidemiological studies on a variety of zoonotic diseases in order to improve public health policies and public attention for One Health concept; also, demonstrated that intestinal bacteria can be used as biomarkers to diagnose Myalgic Encephalomyelitis/Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS) and provide a possible treatment therapy, by analyzing the association between microorganisms and ME/CF.
